**Never start a persistent loop from an exec session inline subshell.** The loop is a child of the exec shell. When the shell exits (as all exec sessions do), SIGHUP kills the loop. ## 20:30–22:00 UTC — P-Table Recalibration (v3): Methodology + Results

### Three bugs found and fixed in `calibrate_p_table.py`
1. **Wrong lead-hours window**: Was using 12–30h (18h lead), should be 30–48h (36h lead) to match actual morning trade timing (~11:15 AM ET for next-day markets). Same 419 matched pairs, different forecast snapshot.
2. **Between-NO missing second tail**: Between-NO wins when actual is OUTSIDE [floor, cap] — two ways to win (above cap OR below floor). Code only counted one tail. Fixed: `P(error > -d or error < -(d+1))` for "above" case; `P(error < d or error > d+1)` for "below" case.
3. **NO trades on greater/less: strict inequality**: For `no/greater`, NO wins when actual ≤ threshold (not strictly <). Since CLI and TWC are both whole °F integers, `error == d` occurs 11.5% of the time at d=2. Fixed to `<=` / `>=`.

### YES/less hypothesis to track
**Thesis**: YES/less trades (e.g., high/yes/less = TWC predicts high below threshold) may have systematically better edge than the p-table implies. Reasons:

1. **TWC is cold-biased here**: For high markets, TWC tends to underestimate highs (+0.49°F mean error = actual runs ABOVE TWC). When TWC says "below threshold," it's fighting its own warm tendency — a stronger signal.

2. **Market adverse selection flips**: For NO trades, the market is skeptical because other (often warmer) models disagree with TWC's warm read. For YES/less, the market is skeptical (pricing YES at 40–55¢) because other models predict WARM — but those warm models are themselves warm-biased. The market may be over-weighting warm forecasts, making YES/less genuinely underpriced.

3. **Live evidence (n=5 unique)**: All 5 unique YES/less trades won. Fill prices were 40–55¢. All deltas (2–5). Too small to conclude, but consistent with the hypothesis.

**What to track going forward:**
- For every YES/less trade: entry fill price, delta, city, and outcome
- Secondary: does the fill price correlate with win rate? (lower fill = more adversarial market = stronger signal or weaker?)
- Target: 20–30 unique YES/less trades before drawing conclusions
- Null hypothesis: win rate is consistent with p-table (68–90% by delta)
- Alternative: win rate meaningfully exceeds p-table, especially at lower fill prices

**Note**: adverse selection for YES trades may also be weaker because YES markets are less liquid and less attention from sophisticated NO-biased traders. Less efficient pricing = more exploitable edge.
