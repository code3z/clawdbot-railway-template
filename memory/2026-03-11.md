## TWC Bias Analysis (afternoon session, 2026-03-11)

### Per-city TWC morning high bias (TWC - CLI actual, n=15-16 days each):
- OKC: -6.3°F mean, ±7.9°F std — most cold-biased but VERY noisy
- Dallas: -4.5°F mean, ±4.6°F std — noisy
- NYC: -2.8°F mean, ±5.1°F std
- Minneapolis: -2.4°F mean, ±6.6°F std
- Austin: -2.1°F mean, ±4.0°F std
- LA (LAX): -2.1°F mean, ±4.2°F std
- Chicago: -2.0°F mean, ±5.2°F std
- SFO: -1.7°F mean, ±2.7°F std
- Seattle: -1.3°F mean, ±2.1°F std
- Las Vegas: +0.9°F (runs slightly WARM)
- DC, Houston, Atlanta: near-zero bias
- Overall: TWC systematically runs cold, especially on warm-surge days

### Bias correction verdict: DO NOT APPLY
- Every city's std is >= its mean bias → not statistically reliable with 15 days of data
- Bias correction creates 91 false positives (fires on days where temp doesn't exceed hi_f)
- The cold bias is non-stationary (regime-dependent, e.g. warm surges fool TWC)
- The raw 1°F upside gate is the right filter — rejects marginal cases where forecast uncertainty dominates
- Revisit when we have 1-2 years of per-city data

- **between_no_above** (TWC above cap): cold bias HELPS — rises to 98% at δ=5+
- **between_no_below** (TWC below floor): cold bias HURTS — plateaus at ~94%

## Evening session (2026-03-11, 7 PM – midnight ET)

### Reprice race condition — `insufficient_balance` bug fixed (commit `bad26d1`, then `0aeca5d`)

**Root cause**: management loop cancel+replace logic re-fetched balance immediately after
`cancel_order()`. Kalshi had not yet released the cancelled order's funds, so the API
returned a stale (too-low) balance. Kelly collapsed → 400 insufficient_balance on
replacement → old order_id stayed in tracking dict as zombie → next poll saw it
"cancelled" → `trade.expire()` called → position silently lost.

**Confirmed affected orders**: NYC T40, plus Philly B37.5, MIN B45.5, AUS T46 (all expired
due to this bug during the 6–7 PM window).

**Fix applied** (commit `0aeca5d`): removed balance re-fetch entirely from reprice block.
Repricing is a price update, not a new sizing decision. Use `o["dollar_size"]` directly.
Removing the Kelly recomputation mid-cancel also eliminated the need for `time.sleep(2)`.

Also added recovery path (commit `bad26d1`): if placement still fails after cancel,
immediately re-place at prior (pre-reprice) limit to stay in market. Simpler and correct.

### Kelly candidate ordering confirmed correct
- Candidates sorted highest-edge-first before placement (line 827: `key=lambda x: -x[1]["edge"]`)
- `available` balance decremented after each order placed
- This IS intentional first-mover bias — highest-edge bets get first claim on capital
- If capital runs out mid-list, weakest bets get dropped. Correct behavior.
